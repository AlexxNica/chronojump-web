Races

Race tests

The following race tests are already defined in the software:
  • Single races: Custom, 20m, 100m, 200m, 400, 1000m, 2000m. Also agility tests: Gesell bar, 20yard, 505, Illinois, Margaria-Kalamen, Shuttle run, ZigZag.
  • Intervallic races: by tracks, by time, unlimited, Modified Time Get Up and Go.
  • You can also define your own race tests.
Results:
  • Time and speed.
Products needed:
  • You need some detection device, commonly photocells. You can put a photocell at start and another at end, or you can put several photocells at different points. Also you can substitute a photocell by an small contact platform (Din-A4 size). Remember to put photocells at the height of the head to avoid eventual double cut problems caused by arms or legs.
  • You will also need Chronojump software and Chronopic microcontroller.
Cables needed:
  • USB cable to connect Chronopic to the computer.
  • Every photocell consist on emitter and receiver (there's no need of cable between them). But for each photocell you need to use one RCA cable in order to connect them. The length of each RCA cable depends on the distance between photocells.
  • Finally you need to merge RCA cables with adaptors in order to connect them to the Chronopic.
  • See the diagram on the right to understand what cables and adaptors you will need. There you can see three situations: 1st a Jump, 2nd a run between two photocells, 3rd a run starting in a contact platform and crossing three photocells. Yellow cables are USB, blue cables are RCA and adaptors (a).
 

Photocells


Velleman PEM10D Photocell.

Features:
  • Response time (5-100 ms)
  • Adjustable sensing range
  • Input voltage AC-DC from 12 to 250V
  • Relay output (pluggable to Chronopic)
Notes:
  • 1 Photocell includes emitter and receiver
  • Use it with Chronopic at 10ms
  • If you don't detect fast movements, adjust response time counterclockwise
